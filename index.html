Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Electrical Engineer, and Futurist

About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electrical systems. He was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia).

Tesla's work laid the foundation for many modern electrical systems and technologies. His inventions include the alternating current power transmission system, the Tesla coil, and the development of wireless transmission of energy.

Early Life and Education

Tesla's fascination with electricity began during his childhood, and he later attended the Technical University of Graz and the Charles-Ferdinand University in Prague. He eventually moved to the United States to work with Thomas Edison and further pursue his innovative ideas.

Theories, Legacy, and Impact

Tesla's theories and inventions revolutionized the field of electricity and engineering. His contributions to AC power transmission played a pivotal role in the adoption of electricity for everyday use. Despite facing financial challenges and controversies, Tesla's vision for wireless communication and free energy continues to inspire scientists, inventors, and researchers.
